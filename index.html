Chapter the First

A Lion, A Tyrant, A Toddler

❦ ⚜ ❦

Prince John is the greedy, thumb-sucking antagonist of Disney's Robin Hood of the year 1973 — an anthropomorphic lion who seizes the crown of England whilst his brother, King Richard, is away upon crusade. He was given voice by Peter Ustinov, whose performance turned a straightforward despot into one of the most entertaining villains the studio ever committed to cel.

What makes him immortal is not his cruelty but his fragility. He hoards gold he has not earned. He raises taxes out of wounded pride rather than policy. He hypnotises his own snake advisor to avoid being told the truth. And the instant the world stops flattering him — a broken mirror, a burning castle, a song sung at his expense — the crown slips down over that pointed head, the thumb goes in, and he cries out for his mother.

He is a man who wants the throne without the crusade, the gold without the governing, and the title without a single one of the obligations attached to it. Which is to say: he was the first memecoin, and we are merely late to the mint.

Chapter the Seventh

Petitions to the Crown

❦ ⚜ ❦
What is $PJ?

A community meme token upon Robinhood Chain, themed after Prince John — the gold-hoarding, thumb-sucking lion of Disney's Robin Hood (1973). It carries no utility beyond memes, vibes, and an entirely unearned sense of monarchy.

What is Robinhood Chain?

Robinhood's Ethereum layer two, built upon the Arbitrum stack, carrying tokenised stocks that trade around the clock and plug into DeFi as collateral. Public mainnet opened on the first of July, 2026, with Uniswap, 1inch and Lighter live on the first day. A fitting realm for a token about robbing from the rich.

Is there a tax?

None upon purchase, none upon sale. Deeply ironic given the source material. The Sheriff of Nottingham has been relieved of his collections.

Is the liquidity safe?

Liquidity is burnt and ownership of the contract renounced. Nobody may drain the treasury — not even the phony king himself.

Is this affiliated with Disney or Robinhood?

It is not. $PJ is an independent, unofficial community project with no affiliation to, endorsement by, or connection with The Walt Disney Company or Robinhood Markets, Inc. All references to the character are parody and fan tribute.
